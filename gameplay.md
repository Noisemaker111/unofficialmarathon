> ## WEAPONS
> 
> ### Overview
> 
> The Marathon trilogy features **14 unique weapons** across three games, with most returning in sequels with upgrades. Weapons fire various ammunition types including bullets, energy bolts, rockets, and napalm.
> 
> **Weapon Control Scheme**:
> - **Primary Fire**: Default attack (single click or continuous)
> - **Secondary Fire**: Alt-fire mode (weapon-dependent)
> - **Reloading**: R key (when applicable)
> - **Weapon Switching**: Number keys 1-8 or scroll wheel
> 
> ### Marathon 1 (1994) Weapons
>

> #### 1. .45 Magnum Mega Class
> 
> **Type**: Semi-automatic pistol  
> **Ammo**: .45 caliber high velocity, vacuum-enabled teflon rounds  
> **Capacity**: 8 rounds  
> **Feed System**: Revolving cylinder  
> **Vacuum Capable**: Yes
> 
> | Stat | Value |
> |------|-------|
> | Damage | ~15-20 (per round) |
> | Fire Rate | Semi-automatic |
> | Accuracy | Highest in game |
> | Range | Long |
> 
> **Primary Fire**: Semi-automatic pistol shots  
> **Secondary Fire**: None
> 
> **Special Properties**:
> - Most accurate weapon in the game
> - **Dual-wieldable** with another Magnum
> - Vacuum-enabled (works in space)
> - Starting weapon
> 
> **Gameplay**: The Magnum serves as the player's starting weapon and workhorse. It is by far the most accurate weapon in the game (except for the rocket launcher) and allows the player to engage targets at range. Though not as effective at close quarters as the assault rifle, the Magnum excels at picking off enemies from a distance.
> 
> **Source**: [.45 Magnum Mega Class - Marathon Wiki](https://www.marathonwiki.com/.45_Magnum_Mega_Class)

> #### 2. M75 Assault Rifle
> 
> **Type**: Fully automatic assault rifle with under-barrel grenade launcher  
> **Ammo**: .75 caliber rounds  
> **Capacity**: 52 rounds (magazine)  
> **Rate of Fire**: 600 RPM  
> **Vacuum Capable**: No
> 
> | Stat | Value |
> |------|-------|
> | Damage | ~10-15 (per round) |
> | Fire Rate | 600 RPM |
> | Accuracy | Low (design defect) |
> | Range | Short-Medium |
> 
> **Primary Fire**: Fully automatic rifle fire  
> **Secondary Fire**: Under-barrel grenade launcher (7 grenades)
> 
> **Grenade Launcher Specs**:
> - Ammo: M-class ultra-high yield grenade cartridges
> - Capacity: 7 grenades
> 
> **Special Properties**:
> - **Grenade jumping**: Explosion propulsion for extra height
> - **Recoil flying**: Can fly through air after initial boost
> - Grenade launcher for area damage
> 
> **Gameplay**: The M75 is the player's main workhorse in the claustrophobic halls aboard the UESC Marathon. Capable of chewing through BOBs and Pfhor fighters with minimal effort with its primary fire, or blowing away crowds with its grenade launcher. The weapon's low accuracy is actually a design feature - not a bug.
> 
> **Source**: [M75 Assault Rifle - Marathon Wiki](https://www.marathonwiki.com/M75_Assault_Rifle)

> **Ammo**: High Energy Bolts  
> **Capacity**: 20 standard energy bolts (5.83 Terawatts each) / 4 charged bolts (17.5 Terawatts)

> **Primary Fire**: Standard high-energy bolts  
> **Secondary Fire**: Overcharge (large 17.5 TW bolt)
> 
> **Special Properties**:
> - Overcharge is independent of battery level (works at 1% charge)
> - Vacuum-enabled
> - **Bonus damage vs. cyborgs** (Marathon 2+ only)
> - Targets killed by fusion bolts can explode for area damage
> 
> **Gameplay**: The weapon of choice for elite soldiers aboard the UESC Marathon. In Marathon (1994), it does NOT deal bonus damage against mechanical enemies - this was only implemented in sequels.

> ## GAMEPLAY SYSTEMS
> 
> ### Health, Shield & Oxygen System
> 
> **Health System**:
> - Player has health pool (typically 100-150 depending on difficulty)
> - Health pickups restore varying amounts
> - Death occurs at 0 health
> 
> **Shield System**:
> - Protective barrier that absorbs damage before health
> - Shield Charge items restore shields
> - Some enemies bypass shields (certain projectiles)
> 
> **Oxygen System**:
> - Underwater sections drain oxygen
> - Oxygen tanks extend underwater survival time
> - Vacuum sections require oxygen or instant death
> - Visual and audio cues when oxygen low
> 
> **Source**: [Marathon Basics - IGN](https://www.ign.com/wikis/marathon-durandal/Marathon_Basics)
> 
> ---
> 
> ### Motion Sensor
> 
> The Motion Sensor is a key gameplay device:
> - Detects movement in the surrounding area
> - Shows enemy positions as blips
> - Range limited (typically 5-10 units)
> - Can be overwhelmed by multiple moving targets
> - Friendly units show differently than enemies
> 
> ---
> 
> ### Terminals
> 
> Terminals are computer interfaces found throughout levels:
> - Provide story exposition and plot information
> - May contain level passwords
> - Reveal map layouts
> - Contain character diaries and logs
> - Sometimes trigger gameplay events
> 
> **Interaction**: Walk up to terminal and press action key to access.
> 
> ---
> 
> ### Teleporters
> 
> **Pattern Buffer**:
> - Standard teleporter system
> - Teleports player to pre-determined destinations
> - May require pattern buffer charge items
> - Can be one-way or round-trip
> 
> **Random Teleporter**:
> - Unpredictable destination
> - Risk/reward for quick traversal
> - May lead to secrets or dangers
> 
> **Level-Specific Teleporters**:
> - Plot-critical teleportation
> - May only work under specific conditions
> 
> ---
> 
> ### Swimming & Underwater Combat
> 
> **Swimming Mechanics**:
> - Movement speed reduced underwater
> - Some weapons malfunction (fusion pistol short-circuits)
> - Oxygen management critical
> - Current effects on movement
> 
> **Underwater Weapons**:
> - Pistol works normally
> - Assault rifle unusable
> - Flamethrower unusable
> - Shotgun may work
> 
> ---
> 
> ### Low-Gravity Mechanics
> 
> **Effects**:
> - Jump height significantly increased
> - Fall damage reduced or eliminated
> - Weapon recoil can propel player
> - Mid-air control reduced
> - Movement momentum carries further
> 
> **Exploitation**:
> - Grenade jumping: Explosions propel player
> - Weapon recoil flying: Sustained fire for transport
> - Flamethrower propulsion: Flame thrust for flight
> 
> ---
> 
> ### Monster Activation & Alarm Systems
> 
> **Activation Triggers**:
> - Visual: Player enters monster's line of sight
> - Audio: Player makes noise (weapons fire)
> - Proximity: Player enters activation radius
> 
> **Alarm Systems**:
> - Certain areas trigger monster hordes when activated
> - Alarm can spread to adjacent areas
> - Some monsters await silently until triggered
> 
> ---
> 
> ### Friendly Fire & Alliance Systems
> 
> **Friendly Fire**:
> - Player can harm allied units
> - Some weapons penetrate through friendlies
> - Grenades and rockets affect all units
> 
> **Alliance States**:
> - Friendly (green): Will not attack player
> - Neutral (yellow): Ignores player unless provoked
> - Hostile (red): Attacks player on sight
>

> ### Difficulty Levels
> 
> The trilogy features multiple difficulty settings:
> 
> | Difficulty | Enemy Damage | Enemy Health | Item Placement |
> |-----------|-------------|--------------|----------------|
> | Kindergarten | Reduced | Reduced | More supplies |
> | Easy | Standard | Standard | Standard |
> | Normal | Standard | Standard | Standard |
> | Hard | Increased | Increased | Fewer supplies |
> | Impossible | Maximum | Maximum | Minimal |
> 
> **Difficulty Scaling**:

> - Enemy health scales with difficulty
> - Item spawns decrease on harder difficulties
> - Some enemy types only appear on higher difficulties

> 
> **Shield Charge**: Restores partial shield  
> **Shield Battery**: Full shield restore  
> **Shield Station**: Full shield restore (requires interaction time)

> 
> **Oxygen Tank**: Extends underwater survival  
> **Oxygen Station**: Refills oxygen (requires interaction time)

>

> - Small: 1 magazine

> - Ammo varies by weapon type

> ### Powerups
> 
> **Invincibility**: Temporary damage immunity (rare)  
> **Invisibility**: Enemies don't detect player (rare)  
> **Speed Boost**: Increased movement speed (rare)

> - Timed switches

> 
> **Keycard Puzzles**:
> - Find colored keycards (Red, Blue, Yellow)

> - Using teleporters correctly
> - Low-gravity jumps to reach new areas

> - Boss encounters
